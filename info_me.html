Rod Bayliss III

Electrical Engineer

I look at Electrical Engineering the way my girlfriend looks at art. She likes the way it makes her feel and understanding a new way to see the world. EE has shown me a new way to look at the world. No matter how huge or complex an electrical system is, you can always break it down into simple elements and work through it to figure out what the system does. Using that simplicity to build real things is a gift I’ve only found in electrical engineering.

That’s the reason I spent days optimizing my CPU for Computation Structures after I’d already gotten an A in the class just to see if I could. That’s why I spend all of my Saturdays sitting in shop designing and soldering my board for FSAE to see my project materialize. Math isn’t hard. Anyone can do electrical engineering because they’re good at it, but you’re not going to find someone who enjoys it more than I do.
